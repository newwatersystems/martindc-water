Case Studies

Restoring Water Service After a System-Wide Outage near Sweetwater, TX

Background:

A rural water system serving over 1,000 connections in a rural area around Sweetwater, TX, experienced a system-wide outage following a prolonged power failure. The outage caused the system's storage tanks to run dry, leaving the entire community without water for over a week. With no water in the lines, the utility faced significant challenges in recharging the system and restoring service.

Challenge:

Recharging a water system after it has run dry requires careful planning to avoid further complications, such as airlock in the pipelines, pressure surges, or contamination risks. The utility lacked the expertise and resources to manage the recharging process efficiently and within a reasonable timeframe.

Solution:

We were brought in to assist with recharging the system. Our team worked quickly to:

  • Evaluate the condition of the storage tanks, pumps, and pipelines to ensure they were ready for reactivation.
  • Develop a systematic plan to refill storage tanks and pressurize distribution lines incrementally, preventing airlocks and pressure shocks.
  • Conduct water quality testing throughout the process to ensure the system remained safe for public use.
  • Provide on-site guidance to utility staff and coordinate with local authorities to keep the community informed about the restoration timeline.

Outcome:

Within 48 hours, the system was fully recharged, and water service was restored to all 1,000+ connections. The careful execution of the recharging process prevented damage to the infrastructure and ensured water quality was maintained. The community regained access to a reliable water supply, and the utility avoided extended downtime and potential regulatory fines.

Conclusion:

This case highlights the importance of expert intervention during emergency situations in rural water systems. With strategic planning and skilled execution, Martin DC helped the Sweetwater system recover quickly, ensuring minimal disruption and long-term system integrity. Reliable emergency response is critical to maintaining trust and service continuity in rural communities.
